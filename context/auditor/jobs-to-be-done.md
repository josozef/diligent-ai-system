1. Long-Term / Annual & Strategic Jobs
A. Design the audit universe & multi-year plan

Define and maintain the audit universe (entities, processes, IT domains, programs) and link it to enterprise and IT risks (via ERM / IT Risk Management). 
Build a multi-year, risk-based audit plan, mapping audits to top enterprise risks, key controls and strategic objectives, using shared risk data from the Diligent One Platform. 
B. Shape the assurance model & “lines of defense”

Define assurance coverage across internal audit, risk and internal controls (who tests what, how often, and at what depth) to avoid overlap and gaps; align this in Diligent’s shared risk/controls libraries. 
Decide where to push testing to first-line owners and where internal audit retains ownership (continuous auditing vs periodic reviews). 
C. Modernize the audit function

Lead digital/AI transformation of internal audit: adopt AI-powered analytics, continuous monitoring, and automation to move from purely retrospective testing to proactive risk insight. 
Standardize methodology and templates (scoping, work programs, rating scales, issue classifications) in Diligent Audit for global consistency. 
D. Strategic stakeholder alignment

Position internal audit as a trusted advisor to management and the board, not just an assurance provider, using connected dashboards and insights across risk, compliance and controls. 
2. Monthly / Quarterly Jobs
A. Risk-based planning & plan refresh

Update the annual audit plan based on new risks, incidents, regulatory changes and management requests, using real-time risk data to reprioritize audits and engagements. 
Present proposed updates to the audit committee and senior management with plan vs actual coverage, using Diligent dashboards and reports. 
B. Execute and oversee audit projects

For each active engagement:

Approve scope, objectives and criteria; ensure linkage to enterprise risks and controls. 
Monitor fieldwork progress, issue identification, and testing coverage, including use of AI analytics for full-population testing and anomaly detection. 
Review draft reports, ensure ratings and issues are calibrated, then finalize and release to stakeholders. 
C. Issue management & remediation oversight

Review and challenge management’s action plans, owners, and due dates for issues raised (including cross-engagement and cross-function issues where IA, compliance and controls intersect). 
Track status of remediation and validate closure; escalate chronic or high-risk overdue items to executives and the audit committee. 
D. Reporting and metrics

Produce quarterly audit committee packs: coverage vs plan, key findings, themes, root causes, and how they link to top risks and controls, leveraging one-click, executive-ready reports in Diligent Audit/Internal Controls. 
3. Weekly / Day-to-Day Jobs
A. Engagement management

Allocate audit resources to engagements, manage workload, and monitor utilization, using project/workflow views in Diligent Audit. 
Review workpaper quality and testing evidence; ensure adherence to methodology and that AI-generated analytics and narratives are used appropriately, not blindly. 
B. Continuous risk and controls insight

Monitor dashboards of risk, control failures and issues across ERM, Internal Controls and Audit to spot hot spots and emerging themes (e.g., repeated control failures in one process or region). 
Adjust audit testing or sampling mid-engagement based on new data or anomalies surfaced by AI analytics. 
C. Coordination with other functions

Coordinate with Risk, Compliance, and IT/security on shared issues, joint reviews and combined assurance efforts (e.g., sharing findings and risk assessments instead of duplicating work). 
D. Stakeholder communication

Hold regular check-ins with management on active audits, clarify expectations, and avoid surprises in final reports. 
4. Emergencies / Incidents & Audit’s Role
Internal audit isn’t first responder, but leaders often have to re-orient plans, provide rapid assurance, and support crisis governance.

A. Major fraud, cyber event, or control breakdown

Jobs:

Reprioritize the audit plan to add or accelerate focused reviews (e.g., emergency audit of the affected process, entity or third-party chain). 
Provide an independent view on:
How the incident occurred given existing controls.
Whether management’s remediation plan is adequate and timely.
Whether similar exposures exist elsewhere (thematic or cross-process reviews). 
Role: crisis-time assurance and insight provider to executives and the board, using Diligent to quickly surface relevant control, risk and issue data.

B. Regulatory or external-audit shock

Jobs:

When a regulator or external auditor flags a serious finding, perform or oversee a targeted internal review of related controls, processes and historical issues using audit and internal controls data. 
Help design and then independently verify remediation, giving the audit committee confidence in management’s response. 
C. Governance crises (e.g., whistleblower, ethics, financial reporting concerns)

Jobs:

Partner with Compliance, Legal and HR to support or lead independent investigations, carefully documenting evidence, scope and conclusions in audit tooling where appropriate. 
Advise the audit committee on control and governance implications, and what additional audits or continuous monitoring are needed going forward. 
For your dashboard, these buckets map well to:

Strategic / Annual: plan, audit universe, coverage vs risk.
Quarterly: plan vs actual, key findings, remediation status, resource utilization.
Weekly / Operational: active engagements, overdue items, hot-spot risks/controls.
Incident / Ad-hoc: “pivot view” that pulls together affected risks, controls, audits and issues to support rapid investigations and plan changes.
